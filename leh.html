How to Reach Ladakh By Road/Air

Ladakh is approx 1050 Kms from Delhi - Manali - Leh. While as Manali to Leh is approx 500 Km. and can be reached by overnight VOLVO. By road, there are two routes to reach Leh. From Srinagar, it is around 434 km travel open to tourists visiting the place from June to November. Another way to tread up the place is via Manali. The Manali-Leh road is a stretch of around 485 km and is open from July to October. Leh Manali Highway is one of the most popular among bikers as the ride involve crossing some very challenging and high mountain passes including second highest motorable pass Taglang La. Ladakh by road is only accessible from Late may to early October as during winter high mountain passes remain closed due to heavy snowfall.

Altitude or Mountain Sickness in Ladakh

What is High Altitude ?

Leh city is located at an altitude of 3500 meter above sea level and this is a high altitude Desert area. Some people feel high altitude sickness on the day of arrival in Leh, therefore this is highly recommended to follow some of the essential tips to avoid high altitude sickness. Avoid travelling for a long distance like Nubra / Pangong Lake / Tso Moriri Lake even lower Indus Valley. Remain in your hotel in Leh and have a complete rest on the day of arrival. This is suggested to have a walk up to Shanti Stupa / Gompa Village to gain height and back to the hotel on bit lower altitude to sleep. Day by day greenery is widening in overall Leh valley which leads increase of Oxygen in the atmosphere

How to Reach Leh Ladakh ?

By Air
This is the quickest way to reach Leh, taking approximately 1 to 2 hours from Delhi.

Flights arrive at Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport (KBT) in Leh, the only airport in the region.

By Train
No Direct Connectivity: There are no direct train services to Leh.

Nearest Stations: You can take a train to Jammu Tawi (about 663 km away) or Banihal (about 510 km away).

By Road
Srinagar-Leh Highway: A 434 km route that passes through Drass and Kargil.

Manali-Leh Highway: A scenic route crossing five high-altitude passes.
