Kerala Tour Package

A Kerala tour offers lush tea gardens in Munnar, serene backwaters in Alleppey, cultural experiences in the port city of Kochi, and the tranquil green forests of Wayanad. Best visited between September and March, typical trips involve exploring these key areas over several days, with activities ranging from houseboat cruises to sightseeing in tea estates and enjoying the local cuisine.

The winter season, from September to March, is considered the best time to visit Kerala to enjoy the pleasant weather and beautiful surroundings.

COCHIN | MUNNAR | THEKKEADY | ALLEPPEY

Day 01: Cochin (35 Kms/1 Hr Drive)

You are greeted on arrival at Cochin Airport/Railway station by our representative and thereafter transfer to the hotel. After refresh continue with the local sightseeing tour ,here you can see the St Francis Church, the oldest Church in India, 500 year old Portuguese Houses, Chinese fishing nets, 16th century Synagogue, Jew Town and Mattancherry Palace, built by Portuguese, this Palace contains some of India’s most beautiful murals. Stay overnight at The Classik Fort - Deluxe A/c. (NB - Jewish synagogue, Mattancherry Palace etc are closed on all Fridays & Saturdays)

Day 02: Munnar (130 Kms/4 Hrs drive)

Morning after breakfast drive to Munnar a hill station(130 Kms/4 Hrs drive), located 1600 meters above sea level. Once the summer resort of the erstwhile British administration in South India it still retains its colonial charm and is famous for its tea plantations, most of which are privately owned. With its sprawling estates, rolling hills, sparkling waterfalls, picture postcard hamlets and undulating valleys, Munnar is an ideal holiday destination. En route visiting Cheyyapara & Valara waterfalls. Check into your hotel and unwind. Rest of the day at leisure to enjoy the ambience of Munnar.

Day 03: Thekkady (110 Kms/3.5 Hrs drive)

Moring after breakfast 9 AM drive to Thekkady(110 Kms/3.5 Hrs drive), an area known for its undulating hills, spice plantations, and elephant sightings. One of the 27 wildlife reserves in India, the Periyar valley in Thekkady is the oldest. Check into your hotel. Afternoon you can go for boat cruise in the Lake to watch wild animals is an option (Inside the sanctuary). Pls make the prior reservation for boating through http://www.ktdc-boating.com/ (preferable timing are 1.45 pm or 3.30 pm). You can also explore the activities like Elephant ride, Spice Plantation tour, Jeep Safari etc,

Day 04: Alleppey (140 Kms/4 Hrs drive)

Another morning drive, this time, to Alleppey, popularly called the Venice of the East, to see the winding canals and an amazing network of backwaters. On arrival, you will be checking in to a traditional Kerala houseboat which will take you on a fabulous tour of the backwaters

Day 05: Departure (30 Min drive)

Departure Transfer to Cochin International Airport/Railway station for onward journey.

How to Reach Kerala ?

By Air
This is the quickest way to reach Leh, taking approximately 1 to 2 hours from Delhi.

Flights arrive at Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport (KBT) in Leh, the only airport in the region.

By Train
No Direct Connectivity: There are no direct train services to Leh.

Nearest Stations: You can take a train to Jammu Tawi (about 663 km away) or Banihal (about 510 km away).

By Road
Srinagar-Leh Highway: A 434 km route that passes through Drass and Kargil.

Manali-Leh Highway: A scenic route crossing five high-altitude passes.
