Bandra-Worli Sea Link

It significantly cuts down commute time between Mumbai's western suburbs and the city center, easing congestion. The bridge is considered a prestigious piece of engineering and an iconic part of Mumbai's landscape. Visitors can take scenic drives across the bridge, enjoying engineering marvels and nighttime views. The Bandra-Worli Sea Link (officially the Rajiv Gandhi Sea Link) is a 5.6 km (3.5 mi), 8-lane cable-stayed bridge in Mumbai, India, connecting Bandra in the west to Worli in the south, reducing travel time from up to 90 minutes to just 10-20 minutes. Opened in 2009, it's an iconic piece of modern infrastructure that alleviates congestion, offers stunning views of the Arabian Sea, and has become a symbol of Mumbai's progress and an attraction for visitors.

How to Reach Mumbai ?

By Air
Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport (BOM): is Mumbai's main airport, serving both domestic and international flights. It is located in the city's suburbs
By Train
Major railway stations in Mumbai include Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CST), a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and Mumbai Central. These stations connect Mumbai to cities across India.
